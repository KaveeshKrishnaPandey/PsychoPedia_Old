Clinical depression
Also called: Major depression
Requires a medical diagnosis
symptoms;
The persistent feeling of sadness or loss of interest that characterises major depression can lead to a range of behavioural and physical symptoms.
These may include changes in sleep, appetite, energy level, concentration, daily behaviour or self-esteem. Depression can also be associated with thoughts of suicide.
People may experience:
1)Mood: anxiety, apathy, general discontent, guilt, hopelessness, loss of interest, loss of interest or pleasure in activities, mood swings, or sadness
2)Behavioural: agitation, excessive crying, irritability, restlessness, or social isolation
3)Sleep: early awakening, excess sleepiness, insomnia, or restless sleep
4)Whole body: excessive hunger, fatigue, or loss of appetite
5)Cognitive: lack of concentration or slowness in activity
6)Weight: weight gain or weight loss
7)Also common: 
#poor appetite #repeatedly going over thoughts #or thoughts of suicide

*****Treatment consists of antidepressants;
The mainstay of treatment is usually medication, talk therapy or a combination of the two. Increasingly, research suggests that these treatments may normalise brain changes associated with depression.
